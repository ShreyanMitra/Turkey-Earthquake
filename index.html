Why should we consider donating or helping in some way?

If you would like to send care packages to Turkey to assist those affected by the earthquake, there are a few things to consider before sending a package. Firstly, it's important to ensure that the items you are sending are actually needed and will be useful to those receiving them. Sending items that are not needed can actually create more work for relief organizations who have to sort through and dispose of them. Secondly, it's important to make sure that the items you are sending comply with any customs regulations or restrictions that may be in place. One option to consider is to donate money to a relief organization that is already on the ground in Turkey. These organizations have the knowledge and experience to determine what items are needed most and how to distribute them effectively. You can also consider donating to a local organization in Turkey that is helping with relief efforts. If you still wish to send a care package, consider contacting a local relief organization in Turkey to see if they are accepting packages and what items are needed. This can help ensure that your package is actually useful and that it reaches those who need it most. Keep in mind that shipping a package internationally can be costly and may take some time to arrive. Make sure to carefully package any items you send and clearly label them with the recipient's information.

What are the effects of this earthquake?

According to worldbank.org, "ANKARA, February 27, 2023—The two very large earthquakes of February 6 caused an estimated $34.2 billion in direct physical damages in Türkiye, the equivalent of 4% of the country's 2021 GDP, according to a World Bank rapid damage assessment report released today". Also, almost 60,000 people have died in this earthquake.
